AI as Search and Optimization

Viewing intelligence as finding optimal solutions in vast spaces

Introduction

One of the classical and most influential views of Artificial Intelligence is that AI is fundamentally a problem of search and optimization. Many intelligent tasks can be framed as searching through a space of possible solutions to find the best one according to a defined objective.

Problem Formulation as Search

In classical AI, a problem is defined by:

  • Initial state – where the agent starts
  • Actions – possible moves from a state
  • State space – all reachable states
  • Goal test – checks if a state is a solution
  • Path cost – cost of reaching a state

Solving the problem means searching the state space efficiently.

Uninformed Search

Uninformed (blind) search algorithms explore the state space without additional domain knowledge.

  • Breadth-First Search (BFS)
  • Depth-First Search (DFS)
  • Uniform-Cost Search
These methods guarantee completeness under certain conditions but can be computationally expensive.

Informed Search and Heuristics

Informed search uses heuristics—estimates of the distance to the goal— to guide exploration.

  • Greedy Best-First Search
  • A* Search

A* search is optimal if the heuristic is admissible and consistent.

Search vs Optimization

Search focuses on finding a solution, while optimization focuses on finding the best solution among many.

Optimization problems involve defining:

  • An objective (fitness) function
  • Constraints
  • A solution space

Local Search and Optimization Algorithms

Local search methods improve a candidate solution iteratively:

  • Hill Climbing
  • Simulated Annealing
  • Tabu Search

These methods scale well to large spaces but may get stuck in local optima.

Evolutionary and Population-Based Methods

Inspired by natural evolution, these methods operate on populations of solutions.

  • Genetic Algorithms
  • Genetic Programming
  • Swarm Intelligence

They balance exploration and exploitation effectively.

Optimization in Machine Learning

Modern AI systems rely heavily on optimization.

  • Training neural networks minimizes loss functions
  • Gradient-based optimization dominates deep learning
  • Regularization introduces constraints

Learning can be seen as searching for parameters that minimize error.

Computational Challenges

Search and optimization often face exponential complexity. Trade-offs between optimality and efficiency are unavoidable.

Approximation and heuristics are essential in real-world AI systems.

Why Search and Optimization Matter

Viewing AI as search and optimization provides a unifying framework across planning, games, robotics, and machine learning.

Even modern AI successes can be traced back to efficient search guided by mathematical objectives.

Conclusion

AI as search and optimization emphasizes that intelligence emerges from efficiently exploring vast possibilities under constraints. While representations and learning matter, the ability to search and optimize remains at the core of intelligent behavior.
