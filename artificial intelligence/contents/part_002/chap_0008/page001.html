Single-Agent Systems and Multi-Agent Systems

Understanding Intelligent Decision Making with One or Multiple Agents

1. Introduction

In Artificial Intelligence, an agent is an entity that perceives its environment through sensors and acts upon that environment through actuators. Depending on how many agents operate within an environment, AI systems can be classified as single-agent systems or multi-agent systems.

This distinction has significant implications for environment modeling, decision making, complexity, and system behavior.

2. Single-Agent Systems

2.1 Definition

A single-agent system is a system in which only one intelligent agent operates in the environment and is responsible for achieving its goals.

2.2 Characteristics

  • No interaction with other intelligent agents
  • Environment behavior depends only on the agent and natural dynamics
  • Simpler modeling and decision making
  • Performance depends solely on the agent’s actions

Example: Maze-Solving Robot

A robot navigating a maze alone to reach a goal position operates as a single-agent system. There are no competing or cooperating agents.

3. Multi-Agent Systems

3.1 Definition

A multi-agent system consists of two or more intelligent agents that interact with each other within a shared environment.

3.2 Characteristics

  • Presence of multiple autonomous agents
  • Agents may cooperate, compete, or coexist
  • Environment dynamics depend on actions of multiple agents
  • Higher complexity and uncertainty

Example: Traffic System

Each vehicle acts as an independent agent. The behavior of one vehicle influences the decisions of others, making traffic a multi-agent system.

4. Types of Multi-Agent Interactions

4.1 Cooperative Systems

Agents work together to achieve a shared goal, such as multiple robots carrying an object collaboratively.

4.2 Competitive Systems

Agents have conflicting goals. Games like chess and poker fall into this category.

4.3 Mixed Systems

Agents may cooperate within teams while competing against other teams, as seen in robot soccer.

5. Comparison: Single-Agent vs Multi-Agent Systems

Aspect Single-Agent Systems Multi-Agent Systems
Number of Agents One Two or more
Interaction No interaction with other agents Agents interact with each other
Complexity Lower Higher
Environment Dynamics Depends mainly on one agent Depends on actions of multiple agents
Examples Single robot, puzzle solving Games, traffic, markets

6. Implications for Agent Design

Designing multi-agent systems requires addressing additional challenges such as:

  • Coordination and communication
  • Negotiation and cooperation
  • Game-theoretic reasoning
  • Handling strategic behavior of other agents

In contrast, single-agent systems can often be solved using classical planning and search techniques.

7. Summary

Single-agent systems involve a lone agent interacting with its environment, while multi-agent systems involve multiple agents whose actions affect each other.

Understanding this distinction is crucial for modeling real-world AI problems, from robotics and games to economics and social systems.
