Stochastic Environments

When actions lead to uncertain or probabilistic outcomes

1. Definition

An environment is stochastic if the outcome of an agent’s action is not completely determined by the current state and action alone.

Instead, outcomes are governed by probability distributions.

Same state + same action → different possible outcomes.

2. Sources of Stochasticity

  • Random events in the environment
  • Noisy actuators or sensors
  • Unpredictable behavior of other agents
  • Incomplete environmental models

3. Stochastic vs Deterministic

Aspect Deterministic Stochastic
Outcome certainty Guaranteed Probabilistic
Uncertainty None Intrinsic
Decision criterion Exact outcome Expected utility
Example Chess Backgammon

4. Examples

Backgammon

Dice rolls introduce randomness into the game.

Stock Market Trading

Market reactions are uncertain and influenced by many hidden factors.

Robotics in the Real World

Slippage, sensor noise, and external disturbances affect outcomes.

5. Implications for Agent Design

In stochastic environments, agents cannot plan based on certainty. Instead, they aim to maximize expected performance.

  • Probabilistic reasoning is essential
  • Utility functions guide decisions
  • Risk-sensitive planning may be required
Rational agents choose actions with the highest expected utility.

6. Formal Models

Stochastic environments are commonly modeled using Markov Decision Processes (MDPs).

MDPs represent probabilistic transitions and rewards over states and actions.

7. Stochastic vs Uncertain

Stochasticity refers to known probability distributions. Uncertainty may also arise when probabilities themselves are unknown.

Learning agents attempt to reduce uncertainty through experience.

8. Real-World Significance

Almost all real-world environments are stochastic to some degree.

  • Weather systems
  • Traffic patterns
  • Human behavior

9. Summary

Stochastic environments introduce uncertainty, requiring agents to reason probabilistically and optimize expected outcomes rather than guaranteed ones.

Intelligence emerges from making good decisions under uncertainty.
